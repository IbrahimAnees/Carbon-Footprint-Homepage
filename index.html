About Carbon Tiptoe

Carbon Tiptoe is a free web-based carbon footprint calculator. Our easy-to-use calculator is suitable for users ranging from individuals to large organizations. Once you have obtained a score, you will be able to see how you compare to other users.

In addition, Carbon Tiptoe provides personalized feedback based on your input that helps improve your carbon footprint. To get started, simply register now!

How does it work?

Carbon Tiptoe asks you a series of questions related to your carbon footprint. Based on your input, Carbon Tiptoe uses a specialized algorithm to calculate your result. We strive to constantly refine our algorithm in order to ensure the most accurate results.

The types of questions asked range from travel habits to waste disposal. We aim to cover every possible aspect in calculating your footprint. Carbon Tiptoe then gives personalized feedback which you can implement to help improve your footprint.

History

The concept and name of the carbon footprint was derived from the ecological footprint concept, which was developed by William E. Rees and Mathis Wackernagel in the 1990s.

The idea of a personal carbon footprint was popularized by a large advertising campaign of the fossil fuel company BP in 2005, designed by Ogilvy. It instructed people to calculate their personal footprints and provided ways for people to "go on a low-carbon diet". This strategy, also employed by other major fossil fuel companies borrowed heavily from previous campaigns by the tobacco industry and plastics industry.
